## Design Context

### Users
Single user (owner), personal internal tool ("work-hub"): Dashboard, DB Manager, Finanzas (personal finances, PEN currency), Configuration. Used on both desktop and phone.

### Brand Personality
Simple y amigable — a personal finance app should feel less like a spreadsheet and more like a friendly personal tool. Color used sparingly on icons/accents for warmth, not a full rebrand.

### Aesthetic Direction
No longer stock shadcn. A single warm terracotta/burnt-orange accent (OKLCH hue 40) was chosen as the brand color — friendly and personal without reading as corporate SaaS blue or AI-cliché indigo/purple. All neutral tokens in `src/index.css` (`:root` and `.dark`) were converted to OKLCH and tinted toward hue 40 at very low chroma for subconscious cohesion; `--primary`/`--ring` carry the accent at full chroma. Scope is still UX polish, not a full redesign — keep future work within this palette rather than introducing new hues.

**Restrained Studio Ghibli direction** (from step 2 onward): warm natural palette (Totoro forest greens, Kiki sunset oranges/reds, sun-faded watercolor rather than saturated cartoon colors), slightly softer geometry, a friendly-but-legible typeface — achieved entirely through existing tokens/CSS properties. No illustrations, no textures, no new decorative elements; this stays a minimalist product UI, not a themed app. Concretely:
- `--background`/`--card`/`--popover` in `:root` nudged from near-white `oklch(0.9911 0.0026 40)` to `oklch(0.984 0.0055 40)` — still very light (L stays ~0.98) but with roughly 2x the chroma so it reads as warm cream paper instead of clinical white. `.dark` got the faintest matching nudge, chroma only (`0.0059` → `0.008`), lightness untouched.
- `--radius` softened from `0.5rem` to `0.7rem` — gentler/rounder corners without tipping into "bubbly/childish" (this is a household finance+memories app, not a kids' app).
- Added a second natural accent, `--success` (muted sage/forest green, Totoro-coded, `oklch(0.6 0.09 145)` light / `oklch(0.72 0.1 145)` dark), lower chroma than `--income` so it doesn't compete with the money convention. Wired only where a genuine generic-default green already existed: Dashboard `MetricCard` trend-up indicator and DB Manager `QueryEditor` success checkmark (both were raw `text-green-500`). Not applied speculatively elsewhere.
- Typeface: **Nunito** (single family, loaded via Google Fonts `<link>` in `index.html`, weights 400/500/600/700 matching actual usage), applied via `--font-sans` in `@theme inline` and `font-sans` on `body`. Chosen for warm/homespun/unhurried — rounded terminals read as hand-lettered-sign-friendly without being a comic/display font, and it stays fully legible at UI sizes (dense tables, form labels, buttons). Rejected the banned-reflex list (Fraunces, Newsreader, Lora, Crimson*, Playfair Display, Cormorant*, Syne, IBM Plex*, Space Mono, Space Grotesk, Inter, DM Sans, DM Serif*, Outfit, Plus Jakarta Sans, Instrument Sans/Serif) and avoided rounded-but-cartoonish options (Fredoka, Baloo, Comic Neue) that would skew "kids' app."
- Type scale/sizing untouched — that's a later pipeline step.

Functional colors: Finanzas income/expense now use dedicated `--income`/`--expense` tokens (OKLCH equivalents of Tailwind green-500/red-500, kept outside the brand accent — money in/out is a universal convention, not a place for brand color). Citas star ratings intentionally keep raw `yellow-400` — ratings are a near-universal UI convention and the chosen accent hue (warm orange, close to yellow) would be visually confusable with it, so this was a deliberate exception, not a leftover default.

### Design Principles
- Mobile is a first-class surface, not a shrunk desktop view: replace dense tables with stacked cards on small screens.
- Respect touch targets (44px min) and thumb reach (primary actions bottom-of-screen: FAB + bottom nav already in place for Finanzas).
- Keep visual weight neutral (60/30/10); use color accents (category icons, income/expense) sparingly to add warmth without a redesign.
- Don't hide functionality on mobile — adapt layout (cards, sheets) rather than cutting features.
